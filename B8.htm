Balm of Gilead (Bot.), A small evergreen African and Asiatic tree of the terebinthine family ({Balsamodendron Gileadense). Its leaves yield, when bruised, a strong aromatic scent; and from this tree is obtained the balm of Gilead of the shops, or balsam of Mecca. This has a yellowish or greenish color, a warm, bitterish, aromatic taste, and a fragrant smell. It is valued as an unguent and cosmetic by the Turks. The fragrant herb Dracocephalum Canariense is familiarly called balm of Gilead, and so are the American trees, Populus

 balsamifera, variety candicans (balsam poplar), and Abies balsamea (balsam fir).
