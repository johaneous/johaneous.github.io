Futuritial (a.) Relating to what is to come; pertaining to futurity; future. [R.]

Futurition (n.) The state of being future; futurity. [R.]

Nothing . . . can have this imagined futurition, but as it is decreed. -- Coleridge.

Futurity (n.) Future time; time to come; the future.

Futurity (n.) Event to come; a future event.

All futurities are naked before the All-seeing Eye. -- South.

Futurity (n.) The time yet to come [syn: future, hereafter, futurity, time to come] [ant: past, past times, yesteryear].

Fuze (n.) [C] 保險絲，熔線；導火線，導火索 A tube, filled with combustible matter, for exploding a shell, etc. See Fuse, n.

Chemical fuze, A fuze in which substances separated until required for action are then brought into contact, and uniting chemically, produce explosion.

Concussion fuze, A fuze ignited by the striking of the projectile.

Electric fuze, A fuze which is ignited by heat or a spark produced by an electric current.

Friction fuze, A fuze which is ignited by the heat evolved by friction. See fuzee [1].

Percussion fuze, A fuze in which the ignition is produced by a blow on some fulminating compound.

Time fuze, A fuze adapted, either by its length or by the character of its composition, to burn a certain time before producing an explosion.

Fuse or Fuze (n.) (Elec.) A wire, bar, or strip of fusible metal inserted for safety in an electric circuit. When the current increases beyond a certain safe strength, the metal melts, interrupting the circuit and thereby preventing possibility of damage. It serves the same function as a circuit breaker.

Fuze (n.) Any igniter that is used to initiate the burning of a propellant [syn: fuse, fuze, fusee, fuzee, primer, priming].

Fuzz (v. i.) To fly off in minute particles.

Fuzzle (v. t.) To make drunk; to intoxicate; to fuddle. [Obs.] -- Burton.
