Stew (v. t.) To boil slowly, or with the simmering or moderate heat; to seethe; to cook in a little liquid, over a gentle fire, without boiling; as, to stew meat; to stew oysters; to stew apples.

Stew (v. t.) A state of agitating excitement; a state of worry; confusion; as, to be in a stew.

Stew (n.) [ C or U ] （通常由肉或魚加蔬菜製成的）燉菜，燜菜 A type of food consisting usually of meat or fish and vegetables cooked slowly in a small amount of liquid.

Examples:

Lamb/bean/fish stew.

She prepared a hearty stew for dinner.

Idiom:

In a stew (informal) 坐立不安；心煩意亂 If someone is in a stew, that person is in a difficult situation that causes them to feel worried or upset.

Examples:

William is in a stew about/over his tax return.

Stew (v.) [ T ] 燉，煨，用文火煮To cook meat, fish, vegetables, or fruit slowly and gently in a little liquid.

Stew (v.) [ I ] (informal) 生氣，發火 To be angry or worried.

Examples:

You're not still stewing about what happened yesterday, are you?

Stew (v.) [ I ] (UK) 消磨時間，無所事事 To do nothing useful.

Examples:

With jobs so scarce, many young people spend long hours with little to do but drink and stew.

Idiom:

Stew (in your own juice) (informal) 反省自己做過的蠢事；自作自受 To think about or suffer the results of your own actions, without anyone giving you any help.
