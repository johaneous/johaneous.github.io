Ferrocyanide (n.) (Chem.) 氰亞鐵酸鹽；亞鐵氰化物 One of a series of complex double cyanides of ferrous iron and some other base.

{Potassium ferrocyanide} (Chem.), Yellow prussiate of potash; a tough, yellow, crystalline salt, {K4(CN)6Fe}, the starting point in the manufacture of almost all cyanogen compounds, and the basis of the ferric ferrocyanate, prussian blue. It is obtained by strongly heating together potash, scrap iron, and animal matter containing nitrogen, as horn, leather, blood, etc., in iron pots.

Ferrocyanide (n.) Salt of ferrocyanic acid usually obtained by a reaction of a cyanide with iron sulphate.

Ferrocyanide (n.) The complex ion Fe(CN)6 4-, used in making blue pigments, blueprint paper, and ferricyanide.

Compare: Ferricyanide

Ferricyanide (n.) 【化】鐵氰化物 The complex ion Fe(CN)6 3- or any of various salts containing it, used in making blue pigments.

Ferricyanide (n.) (Chem.) One of a complex series of double cyanides of ferric iron and some other base.

{Potassium ferricyanide} (Chem.), Red prussiate of potash; a dark, red, crystalline salt, {K6(CN)12Fe2}, consisting of the double cyanide of potassium and ferric iron. From it is derived the ferrous ferricyanate, Turnbull's blue.

Ferricyanide (n.) Salt of ferricyanic acid obtained by oxidation of a ferrocyanide.
