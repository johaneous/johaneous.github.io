Blow (v.) (blew, blown) (SEND OUT AIR) (B1) [ I or T ] 吹；吹動；被吹走 To move and make currents of air, or to be moved or make something move on a current of air.

Examples:

The wind was blowing harder every minute.

The letter blew away and I had to run after it.

A gale-force wind had blown the fence down.

I blew the dust off the books.

I wish you wouldn't blow smoke in my face.​

Blow (v.) (C2) [ I or T ] 吹響；吹奏，演奏 To make a sound by forcing air out of your mouth and through an instrument, or to make a sound when someone does this.

Examples:

Ann blew a few notes on the trumpet.

He scored the winning goal just before the whistle blew.

Blow (v.) [ T ] 吹製（玻璃） To blow air down a tube into a piece of heated soft glass, in order to shape it into an object.

Examples:

A beautiful blown glass vase.

Blow your nose (B1) 擤鼻子 To force air from your lungs and through your nose to clear it.

Blow sb a kiss also blow a kiss to/ at sb向（某人）送飛吻 To kiss your hand and blow on it in the direction of someone.

Blow (v.) (Destroy) [ T ] 毀掉；炸毀 To cause something to be destroyed by a bomb, technical failure, etc..

Examples:

Blow (v.) [ I or T ] （使）（保險絲）燒斷 If an electrical fuse (= a short, thin piece of wire) blows, or if something electrical blows a fuse, the device it is in stops working because it is receiving too much electricity.

Blow (v.) [ I ] ( Informal) （輪胎）爆開，爆裂 If a tyre blows, it suddenly gets a hole in it and goes flat.

Blow sth sky-high 將…炸得稀爛；嚴重損壞 To seriously damage something by making it explode.

Examples:

The explosion blew the building sky-high.

Blow (v.) (Spend) [ T ] (Informal) 揮霍，浪費 To spend a large amount of money, especially on things that are not really necessary.
