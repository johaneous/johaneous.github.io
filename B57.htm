Bludgeon (v.) Overcome or coerce as if by using a heavy club; "The teacher bludgeoned the students into learning the math formulas."

Bludgeon (v.) Strike with a club or a bludgeon [syn: club, bludgeon].

Blue (a.) Having the color of the clear sky, or a hue resembling it, whether lighter or darker; as, the deep, blue sea; as blue as a sapphire; blue violets. "The blue firmament." -- Milton.

Blue (a.) Pale, without redness or glare, -- said of a flame; hence, of the color of burning brimstone, betokening the presence of ghosts or devils; as, the candle burns blue; the air was blue with oaths.

Blue (a.) Low in spirits; melancholy; as, to feel blue.

Blue (n.) Suited to produce low spirits; gloomy in prospect; as, thongs looked blue. [Colloq.]
